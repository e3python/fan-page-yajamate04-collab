About Ivan Cornejo

Iván Cornejo is an American singer-songwriter and guitarist, born June 5, 2004, in Riverside, California, USA. He's known for blending traditional regional Mexican styles with emotional storytelling and alternative influences.

Early Life & Musical Beginnings

Cornejo grew up immersed in music. He taught himself guitar at a young age by watching tutorials on YouTube and began writing songs inspired by personal experiences—including a heartbreak in middle school that later became the emotional core of his music. His early influences included both Mexican regional artists and diverse styles he heard at home.

Style & Impact

Cornejo is widely associated with the so-called "sad sierreño" movement—melancholic, guitar-centric emotionally open songs that blend traditional Mexican music with contemporary sensibilities. His lyrics often explore heartbreak, longing, and personal reflection, which resonates strongly with Gen Z audiences.

His music spans emotional ballads and genre hybrids that feature influences from emo, indie rock, and R&B alongside sierreño and corrido traditions.

Notable Singles & Chart Success

  • "Está Dañada" – Viral hit and breakout song
  • "Baby Please" – A 2024 single that reached the US Hot Latin Songs top 10
  • "Aquí Te Espero", "Dónde Estás," and "Intercambio Injusto" — Tracks with both emotional depth and chart recognition
  • "Me Prometí" – 2025 single that charted on the Billboard Hot 100

Touring & Popularity

Cornejo's live shows have drawn large, sold-out audiences in the U.S.—including multiple nights at major arenas—and his performances are known for emotional connection and musicality. He's built a strong following on platforms like TikTok and Spotify, where his personal, heartfelt style resonates strongly.

Influence & Legacy

At a young age, Iván Cornejo has already become a significant voice in contemporary Latin music, helping expand the reach of regional Mexican genres and connecting deeply with younger fans through vulnerability and genre-blending. His work has helped redefine what música Mexicana can sound like today.
